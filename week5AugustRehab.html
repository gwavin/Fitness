Week 5 — Shoulder + Elbow Rehab with Barefoot Run

Phase 2 begins — Build & Balance
How to use this page
  • Click any exercise to open the full how‑to, cues, mistakes, and regressions/progressions.
  • Pain rule: 0–3/10 is acceptable. Stop for sharp/catching pain or if soreness >24h next day.
  • Week 5 progression: keep lower‑body mains at 3×6–8 @ RPE 6–7. Add joint‑friendly neutral‑grip press and keep chest‑supported rows. Shift elbow work to heavy‑slow resistance (HSR). Run intervals extend to 2‑minute bouts.
Week 5 Defaults
  • Squat (choose one): Back Squat if shoulders are happy 3×6–8 @ RPE 6–7 • OR stay Goblet/FFESS same sets/reps/effort.
  • Hinge: RDL 3×8 light–moderate, perfect technique.
  • Upper: Chest‑Supported Row 3×10–12 • Neutral‑Grip DB Press (slight incline) 3×8–10.
  • Cuff: Band ER 2–3×12 + IR 3×10–12 slow 3s returns.
  • Elbow: Wrist‑extensor HSR 3×10–12 (2–3s up/3s down). Keep a single isometric calm set only if flared.
  • Run sessions: 6–8× (2:00 jog / 1:00–2:00 walk) between 5′ warm‑up/cool‑down. If next‑morning calf/Achilles stiffness >60 min, repeat same volume.
  • Grip neutral where possible; straps allowed on hinge work if elbow talks.

Week 5 – Alternating Days (Weights ↔ Run) + Daily Shoulder Rehab

Progress only when next‑day symptoms are ≤3/10. Use the buttons below to expand/collapse details.

Monday — Weights + Elbow HSR (~45–50 min)

Open Books (T‑spine) — 6 reps/side
  • Side‑lying; hips/knees 90°. Reach top hand to ceiling, then rotate open toward floor behind you while knees stay stacked. Exhale into range.
  • Stop before any shoulder pinch.
Squat — choose one — 3×6–8 @ RPE 6–7 • 3‑1‑1 tempo
  • Option A — Back Squat (only if shoulders are happy):
  • Rack & grip: J‑hooks set so you can unrack without tip‑toes. Hands as wide as needed for comfortable shoulders; wrists neutral (don’t crank back).
  • Bar & scaps: High‑bar on upper traps (not neck). Gently depress (down) and slightly retract shoulder blades; think "wide collarbones".
  • Stance & brace: Feet ~shoulder‑width, toes slightly out. Big breath into belly/sides; ribs stacked over pelvis.
  • Descent: Sit between hips; knees track over mid‑foot; chest tall. Control ~3s down; brief 1s pause if you tend to bounce.
  • Ascent: Drive the floor; keep knees tracking; eyes fixed. Exhale through the sticking point; finish tall without shrugging.
  • Depth: Aim for hip crease near knee height only if pain‑free. If any shoulder/wrist discomfort from the bar, cut depth slightly and/or switch to Goblet/FFESS.
  • Option B — Goblet or FFESS (front‑foot‑elevated split squat):
  • Goblet: DB/KB at chest; elbows under bell; forearms vertical. Sit down/forward between hips; heels down; 3‑1‑1 tempo.
  • FFESS: Front foot on 2–4" plate/step; rear foot back. Drop straight down; slight forward torso lean; front shin roughly vertical; drive through mid‑foot to stand.
  • Range control: Use a box/bench target if depth consistency helps; stop before lumbar rounding.
  • Common mistakes: knees diving in, heels lifting, rib flare/over‑arch, bar rolling, elbows cranked into painful wrist extension.
  • Pain rules: Shoulder pinch from bar position or >3/10 pain → switch to Goblet/FFESS immediately.
Romanian Deadlift (DB/KB or Bar) — 3×8 light–moderate
  • Soft knees; push hips back; spine neutral; lats packed; weights skim thighs; stop mid‑shin. 2s up.
  • Use straps if grip/elbow complain; neutral wrists.
Chest‑Supported Row (neutral grip) — 3×10–12
  • Bench 30–45°; chest down; palms facing. Depress/retract slightly; row elbows 30–45° from body; pause 1s; lower 2–3s. Neck long; no shrug.
  • Cranking with biceps; shoulders hiking; elbows flared to 90°.
Neutral‑Grip DB Press (slight incline) — 3×8–10
  • Setup: Bench at 15–30°. Feet planted; glutes lightly on bench; ribs gently down. DBs start above lower chest with palms facing (neutral). Shoulders set down/back without arching.
  • Arm path: Elbows travel ~30–45° from torso (not flared). Forearms vertical at mid‑range; wrists straight (no bend back).
  • Range: Lower until before any anterior shoulder pinch or scap winging—often DBs will hover 1–2 cm above chest early on. It’s fine to use partial ROM.
  • Tempo & breath: 2–3s down → light pause/hover → press up while exhaling; finish with a subtle protraction (don’t shrug).
  • Regressions: Raise the incline; shorten ROM; DB floor press (natural stop at upper arm on floor).
  • Progressions: Lower the incline over weeks; micro‑load; add a 1–2s pause at bottom if pain‑free.
  • Elbows >60° from body, rib‑cage flare, shrugging at lockout, bouncing DBs off chest, wrists bent into extension.
Band External Rotation (ER) — 2–3×12 (slow 3s return)
  • Setup: Anchor band at elbow height on the opposite side. Roll a small towel between elbow and ribs (keeps ~5–10 cm space). Stand tall; ribs down; shoulder blade gently set.
  • Motion: Rotate forearm out keeping elbow glued; humeral head stays centered (no shoulder roll forward). Stop before any pinch; avoid trunk twist.
  • Tempo: ~1–2s out • 3s slow return. Breathe smoothly; neck relaxed.
  • Options: If clean, try slight abduction (elbow supported at ~45°) or side‑lying ER with a light DB.
  • Elbow drifting away, shoulder hiking, wrist bending, rotating your torso to cheat.
Band Internal Rotation (IR) — 3×10–12 (slow 3s return)
  • Setup: Anchor band at elbow height on the same side. Towel between elbow and ribs; stand tall; gentle scap set; wrist neutral.
  • Motion: Rotate forearm in toward belly with elbow glued; keep shoulder centered—no rolling the shoulder forward.
  • Tempo: ~1–2s in • 3s slow return. Small, smooth arc beats big sloppy reps.
  • Range: Only to a comfortable stop; avoid pressing into painful end‑range.
  • Torso twisting, elbow drifting, wrist flexing/ulnar‑deviating, upper‑trap shrug.
Wrist‑Extensor HSR — 3×10–12 (2–3s up / 3s down)
  • Setup: Sit with forearm supported on bench/thigh, palm down, wrist just off the edge; elbow ~90° by your side; shoulder relaxed. Hold DB so fingers aren’t death‑gripping; a towel under forearm can improve comfort.
  • Motion: Start near neutral wrist. Lift in 2–3s to a comfortable extension (~15–30°), then lower in 3s back to neutral/slight flexion. Keep movement at the wrist—no shoulder hitching.
  • Load target: Last 1–2 reps feel challenging but crisp (RPE ~7–8) with ≤2–3/10 tendon discomfort and no next‑day spike.
  • Progression: First add reps up to the top of the range, then micro‑load (≈0.5–1 kg). Progress only if next‑day symptoms are ≤3/10 and fade within 24 h.
  • Frequency: 3×/week here; keep at least 48 h between sessions if tendon is irritable.
  • Tingling/nerve symptoms, sharp lateral elbow pain, compensating with shoulder/lats, gripping the DB so hard the wrist can’t move smoothly.
Optional Calm Set (Isometric) — 1×20–30s @ 3–4/10
  • Back‑of‑hand press into wall with elbow at 90°, wrist neutral or ~15° extension. Match pressure so the wrist doesn’t move; breathe.

Tuesday — Barefoot Walk–Jog + Foot/Calf Prep (~30 min)

Short‑Foot Drill — 3×20s/side
  • Gently lift arch (ball of big toe draws toward heel) without curling toes; breathe and relax the ankle.
Double‑Leg Calf Raise — 3×12 slow
  • Full range up/down 2–3s each; rise over big/second toe; brief pause at top; no bouncing.
Tibialis Raise — 2×15
  • Back to wall; heels down; lift toes toward shins; control the lower; knees soft.
Run – Week 5 Session A — 5’ walk → 6–8× (2:00 jog / 1:00–2:00 walk) → 5’ walk longer bouts
  • Cadence ~170–180; land under center; quiet feet; posture tall with slight forward lean from ankles.
  • If next‑morning calf/Achilles stiffness >60 min, repeat same volume before progressing.
Daily Shoulder Block (quick) — Scap Set • ER/IR • Wall Angels
  • Keep movements easy and smooth; stop before any pinchy range.
