Perfected Soured-Milk Waffles

Ingredients (4 round waffles)

  • 1 ¾ cups (225 g) all‑purpose flour optional: replace ¼ cup with cornstarch for extra crispness
  • 2 Tbsp (25 g) granulated sugar
  • 1 ½ tsp (6 g) baking powder
  • ¼ tsp (1 g) baking soda
  • ½ tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 ¾ cups (415 mL) whole or 2 % milk
    • Souring step: Stir in 1 Tbsp (15 mL) fresh lemon juice or white vinegar, then let stand 5 minutes until slightly thickened.
  • 2 large eggs, separated
  • 4 Tbsp (60 g) unsalted butter, melted & cooled Cooled to prevent scrambling the eggs!
  • 1 tsp (5 mL) pure vanilla extract
  • Optional mix‑ins: a handful of mini chocolate chips, blueberries, or toasted pecans (< ½ cup total)

Equipment

  • Cuisinart 2‑in‑1 Waffle & Pancake Maker
  • Two mixing bowls + whisk
  • Hand mixer (or vigorous whisk) for egg whites
  • Measuring cups & spoons, ladle (~⅔ cup capacity)
  • Wire rack for finished waffles

Method

  1. Preheat: Set the Cuisinart to WAFFLE → Medium‑High (about 4 of 5). Close the lid and allow at least 5 minutes to heat. Lightly oil the plates.
  2. Combine dry: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. Mix wet: In a second bowl, whisk together the egg yolks, melted butter, vanilla, and the soured milk.
  4. Combine & Rest: Pour the wet mixture into the dry. Stir just until you no longer see streaks of dry flour (a few lumps are fine). **Cover the bowl and let the batter rest at room temperature for 15-30 minutes.** This step is key for a tender waffle!
  5. Whip Whites: While the batter rests, beat the egg whites in a clean, dry bowl until they hold stable **medium peaks** (the tips should curl over slightly).
  6. Fold: Gently fold the whipped egg whites into the rested batter in two additions, being careful not to deflate the mixture.
  7. Cook: Ladle ~⅔ cup of batter onto the center of the waffle plate, close the lid, and cook for 3 ½–4 minutes, or until the steam subsides and the waffle is golden brown. Add 30 seconds if needed for deeper color.
  8. Hold/crisp: Transfer finished waffles to a wire rack (ideally set inside a baking sheet) in a 95 °C (200 °F) oven for up to 20 min while you finish the batch.
  9. Serve: Sprinkle with powdered sugar and top with warm maple syrup, perhaps mixed with a spoonful of crème fraîche. Fresh berries are a great addition.

Make‑Ahead Tips

  • Freeze cooled waffles in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer bag. Reheat from frozen in a toaster on a medium setting for perfect crispness.
  • The finished batter keeps for 24 hours in the fridge. Note that it will deflate slightly, resulting in a slightly less airy waffle.
