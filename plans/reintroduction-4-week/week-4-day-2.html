Week 4 Day 2: Tuesday

Strength A. Week 4 focus: Consolidate. Maintain moderate loads and aim for consistency. Still no maxing. Success means joints feel stable, karate feels smoother, and energy is improving.

Morning session

Strength A

Warm-up - 5 minutes

  • 1 minute easy marching, step-ups, or light bike.
  • 10 bodyweight good mornings.
  • 10 slow comfortable squats.
  • 10 scapular wall slides or band pull-aparts.
  • 10 calf raises.
  • 5 gentle reverse lunges each side only if the knee feels fine.

Main work

ExercisePrescriptionCues
Box squat or comfortable back squat2-3 sets of 5Start light. Use a box or target height if useful. Smooth reps only. Do not chase depth if the knee is irritated. Knees track roughly in line with toes.
Dumbbell bench press or floor press2-3 sets of 8Choose the shoulder-friendly version. Floor press if the shoulder feels vulnerable. Elbows about 30-45 degrees from the body. Stop before shoulder pinch.
One-arm dumbbell row2-3 sets of 10 each sideSupport the non-working hand. Pull elbow towards hip. Pause briefly at the top. Do not twist or heave.
Romanian deadlift2 sets of 8Light barbell or dumbbells. Soft knees. Hinge from hips. Stop when hamstrings are comfortably loaded. Neutral back.
Suitcase carry2 carries of 30-45 seconds each sideWalk slowly. Stay tall. Do not lean away from the weight.
Evening

15-minute shoulder/knee prehab.

Shoulder / scapula
  • Band external rotations - 2 x 12 each side.
  • Scapular wall slides - 2 x 8-10.
  • Band pull-aparts - 2 x 12.
  • Serratus push-up plus - 1-2 x 8-10.
Knee / ankle / hip
  • Spanish squat hold - 2 x 20-30 seconds, or wall sit if no band.
  • Low step-downs - 2 x 6 each side.
  • Calf raises - 2 x 12.
  • Tibialis raises against wall - 2 x 12.
  • Glute bridge - 1-2 x 10.
Safety: continue for mild stiffness that improves during warm-up. Reduce load, range, or volume for knee discomfort above 3/10, shoulder pinch, elbow pain with gripping or pressing, or next-day soreness affecting walking, stairs, or karate. Stop the movement for sharp pain, giving way, new or worsening swelling, limping, night pain, neurological symptoms, or pain that worsens as the session continues.
Avoid
  • No max testing, grinders, HIIT, sprinting, plyometrics, or hard hill work.
  • No heavy squats or deadlifts.
  • No high-volume push-ups or extra high-volume kicking drills outside karate.
  • Keep pressing conservative and shoulder-friendly.
