Week 3 Day 7: Sunday

Rest, Walking and Prehab. Week 3 focus: Small loading. Add 2.5-5 kg to barbell lifts only if there has been no increase in knee swelling, foot swelling, shoulder pain, elbow pain, limping, or karate-related soreness. Keep pressing conservative.

Morning session

Rest, Walking and Prehab

Warm-up - 5 minutes

  • 1 minute easy marching, step-ups, or light bike.
  • 10 bodyweight good mornings.
  • 10 slow comfortable squats.
  • 10 scapular wall slides or band pull-aparts.
  • 10 calf raises.
  • 5 gentle reverse lunges each side only if the knee feels fine.

Main work

Rest, walking, and prehab only. Do not use Sunday to make up missed hard training.

Evening

Gentle prehab only if useful. Keep it restorative.

Shoulder / scapula
  • Band external rotations - 2 x 12 each side.
  • Scapular wall slides - 2 x 8-10.
  • Band pull-aparts - 2 x 12.
  • Serratus push-up plus - 1-2 x 8-10.
Knee / ankle / hip
  • Spanish squat hold - 2 x 20-30 seconds. See detailed setup below.
  • Low step-downs - 2 x 6 each side.
  • Calf raises - 2 x 12.
  • Tibialis raises against wall - 2 x 12.
  • Glute bridge - 1-2 x 10.
Spanish squat - detailed setup
  1. Anchor a strong band or strap around a solid rack post at knee height.
  2. Step inside the loop so the band sits behind both knees.
  3. Walk back until there is firm band tension pulling the knees forward.
  4. Stand with feet about shoulder-width apart.
  5. Sit back into a squat while keeping the torso fairly upright.
  6. The band supports the knees and allows the shins to stay more vertical.
  7. Hold a comfortable squat depth. You should feel the quadriceps working.
  8. The knee should feel loaded but not sharp or irritated. Keep heels down. Knees track in line with toes.
  9. Start shallow if needed. Do not chase depth.
  10. Stop if pain rises above 3/10 or if swelling is worse the next day.

Alternative if no band or strap: wall sit - 2 holds of 20-30 seconds. Back against wall, feet forward, comfortable knee angle. Do not go too deep initially.

Safety: continue for mild stiffness that improves during warm-up. Reduce load, range, or volume for knee discomfort above 3/10, shoulder pinch, elbow pain with gripping or pressing, or next-day soreness affecting walking, stairs, or karate. Stop the movement for sharp pain, giving way, new or worsening swelling, limping, night pain, neurological symptoms, or pain that worsens as the session continues.
Avoid
  • No max testing, grinders, HIIT, sprinting, plyometrics, or hard hill work.
  • No heavy squats or deadlifts.
  • No high-volume push-ups or extra high-volume kicking drills outside karate.
  • Keep pressing conservative and shoulder-friendly.
