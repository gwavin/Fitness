Gentle 4-Week Exercise Reintroduction Plan

A practical return-to-training plan for rebuilding consistency and tissue tolerance around Monday and Thursday Shotokan karate. Morning sessions fit roughly 06:05–06:45; evening prehab takes 15 minutes. Use RPE 5–6, stop each set with about 4–5 good reps in reserve, and finish sessions with fuel left in the tank. The first month is about rebuilding tolerance, not testing fitness.

Personal training guidance for your own use — not medical advice. Adjust or stop movements based on how your joints respond.

Main principle
  • No max testing, grinders, HIIT, sprinting, or heavy loading in the first four weeks.
  • Karate already provides impact, pivots, sudden loading, balance demands, and skill work.
  • Strength days are Tuesday and Friday only — not Monday or Thursday.
  • Do not use Sunday to make up missed hard training.

Weekly structure

Repeat this pattern for four weeks, adjusting load only according to the progression rules below.

Morning warm-up (every session, 5 minutes)

  • 1 minute easy marching, step-ups, or light bike.
  • 10 bodyweight good mornings.
  • 10 slow comfortable squats.
  • 10 scapular wall slides or band pull-aparts.
  • 10 calf raises.
  • 5 gentle reverse lunges each side — only if the knee feels fine.
Tuesday morning

Strength A

Purpose: squat pattern, push, pull, light hinge, trunk.

Exercise Prescription Cues
Box squat or comfortable back squat 2–3 sets of 5 Start light. Use a box or target height if useful. Keep reps smooth. Do not chase depth if the knee is irritated. Knee tracks roughly in line with toes.
Dumbbell bench press or floor press 2–3 sets of 8 Choose the version the shoulder likes. Floor press preferred if the shoulder feels vulnerable. Elbows about 30–45° from the body. Stop before any shoulder pinch.
One-arm dumbbell row 2–3 sets of 10 each side Support the non-working hand on a bench or rack. Pull elbow towards hip. Pause briefly at the top. Do not twist or heave.
Romanian deadlift 2 sets of 8 Light barbell or dumbbells. Soft knees. Hinge from hips. Stop when hamstrings are comfortably loaded. Back remains neutral. Not a heavy deadlift session.
Suitcase carry 2 carries of 30–45 seconds each side Hold one dumbbell or kettlebell at your side. Walk slowly. Stay tall. Do not lean away from the weight. Excellent for trunk, grip, hip, and gait control.
Friday morning

Strength B

Purpose: hinge pattern, single-leg control, shoulder-safe press, upper back, trunk.

Exercise Prescription Cues
Deadlift from floor or raised blocks 3 sets of 3–5 Start very light — around 60–80 kg if that feels easy and technically crisp. Use raised blocks if the floor pull feels too demanding. No grinders. Stop every rep fully. Keep the bar close. Crisp practice, not load.
Reverse lunge or shallow split squat 2 sets of 6 each side Prefer reverse lunges if the knee is sensitive. Keep the range shallow at first. Front knee tracks in line with toes. Lightly hold the rack for balance if needed. Stop if there is sharp pain or next-day swelling.
Incline push-up or dumbbell press 2–3 sets of 8 Choose the most shoulder-friendly option. Incline push-ups with hands on a bench or rack. Keep body rigid. Avoid deep shoulder stretch.
Band row, chest-supported row, or dumbbell row 3 sets of 10 Focus on upper back and scapular control. Smooth pull. No shrugging. No jerking.
Side plank 2 sets of 20–30 seconds each side Keep ribs down. Hips forward. Stop before form collapses.
Saturday (optional)

Light whole-body circuit

Use only if feeling good after the week. This should feel easy and athletic, not like punishment. Do 2–3 rounds:

  • Goblet squat — 8 reps.
  • Kettlebell deadlift or light Romanian deadlift — 10 reps.
  • Dumbbell floor press or incline push-up — 8–10 reps.
  • One-arm row — 10 reps each side.
  • Farmer carry — 30–45 seconds.
  • Dead bug — 6–8 reps each side.
Easy cardio days

Monday, Thursday, and optionally Wednesday or Saturday mornings

Options: easy bike; brisk walk; gentle walk/jog only if foot and knee are calm; light mobility flow.

Target: 20–30 minutes easy, conversational pace. No HIIT, sprinting, or hard hill work in the first month. If karate was intense, keep the next morning easier.

Evening prehab (15 minutes, every evening)

Daily joint-maintenance routine: shoulder/scapula (~7 min), knee/ankle/hip (~7 min), final minute easy breathing or gentle stretch.

Shoulder / scapula (~7 min)
  1. Band external rotations — 2 sets of 12 each side. Elbow tucked by side. Rotate forearm outward. Keep shoulder blade gently set. No twisting the trunk. Light band only.
  2. Scapular wall slides — 2 sets of 8–10. Back near wall. Ribs down. Slide arms upward without shrugging. Only comfortable range. Stop if shoulder pinches.
  3. Band pull-aparts — 2 sets of 12. Arms roughly chest height. Pull band apart by moving shoulder blades back and slightly down. Do not arch the lower back. Keep reps slow.
  4. Serratus push-up plus — 1–2 sets of 8–10. Against a wall, bench, or floor. Elbows straight. Let shoulder blades move apart at the top. Controlled shoulder blade movement, not a normal push-up.
Knee / ankle / hip (~7 min)
  1. Spanish squat hold — 2 holds of 20–30 seconds. See detailed setup below.
  2. Low step-downs — 2 sets of 6 each side. Low step only. Stand on one leg. Slowly tap the opposite heel to the floor. Keep pelvis level. Knee tracks over toes. Use a hand on the rack or wall for balance.
  3. Calf raises — 2 sets of 12. Rise smoothly. Pause briefly at the top. Lower under control. Progress to single-leg only if both sides are comfortable.
  4. Tibialis raises against wall — 2 sets of 12. Back against wall, feet a little forward, heels on floor. Lift toes towards shins. Lower slowly.
  5. Glute bridge — 1–2 sets of 10. Feet flat. Squeeze glutes to lift hips. Do not overarch the lower back. Pause briefly at the top.
Spanish squat — detailed setup
  1. Anchor a strong band or strap around a solid rack post at knee height.
  2. Step inside the loop so the band sits behind both knees.
  3. Walk back until there is firm band tension pulling the knees forward.
  4. Stand with feet about shoulder-width apart.
  5. Sit back into a squat while keeping the torso fairly upright.
  6. The band supports the knees and allows the shins to stay more vertical.
  7. Hold a comfortable squat depth. You should feel the quadriceps working.
  8. The knee should feel loaded but not sharp or irritated. Keep heels down. Knees track in line with toes.
  9. Start shallow if needed. Do not chase depth.
  10. Stop if pain rises above 3/10 or if swelling is worse the next day.

Alternative if no band or strap: wall sit — 2 holds of 20–30 seconds. Back against wall, feet forward, comfortable knee angle. Do not go too deep initially.

Progression over four weeks

Week 1 — Restart

Use 2 sets for most exercises. Keep everything easy. Prioritise movement quality. No soreness hunting.

Pain and safety rules

Signal Action Examples
Green — continue Proceed as planned Mild stiffness that improves during warm-up; normal muscle soreness; symmetrical tiredness; no increase in swelling.
Amber — reduce Reduce load, range, or volume Knee discomfort above 3/10; shoulder pinch; elbow pain with gripping or pressing; foot or ankle soreness that changes movement; next-day soreness affecting walking, stairs, or karate.
Red — stop and reassess Stop that movement Sharp pain; giving way; new or worsening swelling; limping; night pain; numbness, weakness, or neurological symptoms; pain that worsens as the session continues.
What to avoid in the first four weeks
  • Heavy squats or deadlifts.
  • Max-effort bench or overhead press.
  • HIIT, sprinting, or plyometrics.
  • Long barefoot walks.
  • High-volume push-ups or high-volume kicking drills outside karate.
  • Hard training the morning after a difficult karate session.
Minimum viable fallback (~20 minutes)

On busy or tired days, use this instead of skipping completely:

  • 5-minute warm-up (as above).
  • Goblet squat — 2 sets of 8.
  • Dumbbell floor press — 2 sets of 8.
  • One-arm row — 2 sets of 10 each side.
  • Romanian deadlift — 2 sets of 8.
  • Calf raises — 2 sets of 12.
