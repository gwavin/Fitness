8 pm Shoulder Ritual

A simple indoor evening routine for shoulder mobility, control, and a little upper-arm polish. Designed to be easy enough to do every night without interfering with the morning plan.

Base Rule

Do This Every Evening

  • Keep it to 10-15 minutes.
  • Everything should feel smooth, controlled, and non-irritating.
  • Finish feeling better than when you started.
  • If tired, do the minimum version and still count it.
Intent: mobility, activation, light strengthening, and shoulder insurance - not fatigue or punishment.
Equipment

Use Indoors

  • Light dumbbell
  • 7 kg kettlebell first, 12 kg only if clearly comfortable
  • Resistance bands

Good fit for this routine

  • Band pull-aparts
  • Band external rotation
  • Face pulls
  • Wall slides
  • Light lateral raises
  • Kettlebell rack or waiter-style hold
Default 12-Minute Routine

Base Shoulder Sequence

Movement Prescription Purpose

Use the lightest option that feels clearly smooth. Do not force loaded mobility.

Minimum Version

Tired Night Fallback

If you are tired, busy, or not in the mood, do this and keep the habit intact:

  • Band pull-aparts x 15
  • Band external rotations x 12 each side
  • Wall slides x 8
  • Light lateral raises x 12
Rule: never break the habit just because you cannot do the full version.
Vanity Add-On

Use 2-3 Evenings Per Week

Add one or two of these after the base routine if you feel fresh:

  • Dumbbell curl - 2 x 10-15
  • Hammer curl - 2 x 10-12
  • Rear delt raise - 2 x 12-15
  • Band triceps pressdown - 2 x 12-20

Keep these easy to moderate. Do not turn the evening ritual into a hard hypertrophy session.

Weekly Pattern

Simple Structure

  • Every evening: base shoulder ritual
  • Monday / Thursday / Saturday: add curls and rear delts if desired
  • All other evenings: base work only

How it should feel

  • smooth
  • restorative
  • slightly satisfying
  • not draining
Avoid

Things Not to Do

  • Heavy upright rows
  • Aggressive stretching into pain
  • Lots of overhead pressing at night
  • Deep fatigue on lateral raises
  • Unstable kettlebell shoulder work when tired
Bottom Line

What This Ritual Is For

  • Protect the shoulder
  • Improve movement quality
  • Support morning benching and hanging
  • Add a little delt and arm work
  • Create a dependable nightly habit
Best test: if the shoulder feels better or unchanged the next morning, the routine is pitched correctly.

Knee Resilience Block

A 10-15 minute indoor knee-strengthening block designed to build quadriceps, glute, hamstring, calf and balance capacity without turning the evening routine into a full leg workout.

Practical Setup

Frequency and Equipment

  • Frequency: 3 evenings per week
  • Duration: 10-15 minutes
  • Equipment: chair, wall, stairs or stable step, towel, optional dumbbell or kettlebell
  • Timing: after the shoulder routine, before stretching or mobility

This is a general strengthening and resilience add-on, not a diagnosis or injury treatment plan.

Safety Rule

Keep Stop Rules Visible

Mild discomfort up to 2-3/10 is acceptable if it settles quickly. Stop or regress for sharp pain, increased swelling, giving way, locking, or next-day deterioration.

Seek GP or physio review for persistent swelling, instability, locking, inability to fully straighten, or worsening pain.

Regression Options

If Sore

  • Reduce step height.
  • Reduce the range of the wall sit.
  • Remove load.
  • Replace sit-to-stand with quad squeezes.
  • Perform one round only.
Progression

Weeks 1-2: Build the Base

  • Chair sit-to-stand, shallow wall sit, and low step-up
  • Glute bridge, calf raise, and balance
  • No jumping, twisting, deep squats, or lunges

Weeks 3-4

Add goblet-loaded sit-to-stand or longer wall sits or slower step-downs. Progress only one variable at a time.

Progression

Weeks 5+: Optional Additions

  • Shallow reverse lunge
  • Lateral step-down
  • Slow split squat
  • Single-leg Romanian deadlift

Avoid pivot drills until the knee has been consistently symptom-free and reliable.

Progression Rules

Progress Only When

  • There is no increased swelling, sharp pain, or giving way.
  • Symptoms are no worse the next day.
  • Technique remains controlled.
Conservative rule: change only one variable at a time, such as reps, hold time, range, step height, or load.

Gentle 4-Week Exercise Reintroduction Plan

A practical first-month reset for rebuilding consistency and tissue tolerance around karate, shoulder/scapular history, elbow irritability, knee stiffness, previous ankle fracture, and previous right foot septic arthritis. The goal is to finish sessions with fuel left in the tank, not to test fitness.

Main Principle

First Month Intensity Cap

  • Use RPE 5-6 for the first 4 weeks.
  • Stop each set with about 4-5 good reps still in reserve.
  • No max testing, grinders, HIIT, sprinting, or heavy loading.
  • Karate already covers impact, pivots, sudden loading, balance demands, and skill work.
Training Windows

Available Time

  • Morning sessions: approximately 06:05 to 06:45.
  • Evening prehab: 15 minutes each evening.
  • Karate: Monday and Thursday evenings.

This is a personal training plan, not medical advice. If symptoms escalate or feel unusual, reassess rather than pushing through.

Evening Prehab

15 Minutes Each Evening

  • Shoulder/scapula: about 7 minutes.
  • Knee/ankle/hip: about 7 minutes.
  • Final minute: easy breathing or gentle stretch.

Shoulder/scapula routine

  • Band external rotations: 2 x 12 each side. Elbow tucked, light band, no trunk twist.
  • Scapular wall slides: 2 x 8-10. Ribs down, no shrugging, comfortable range only.
  • Band pull-aparts: 2 x 12. Arms at chest height, shoulder blades back and slightly down.
  • Serratus push-up plus: 1-2 x 8-10 against wall, bench, or floor. Keep elbows straight and control shoulder blade movement.
Evening Prehab

Knee, Ankle, and Hip Routine

  • Spanish squat hold: 2 holds of 20-30 seconds.
  • Low step-downs: 2 x 6 each side.
  • Calf raises: 2 x 12.
  • Tibialis raises against wall: 2 x 12.
  • Glute bridge: 1-2 x 10.
Progress calf raises to single-leg only if both sides are comfortable. For step-downs, use a hand on the rack or wall and prioritise quality over depth.
Spanish Squat Detail

Setup and Stop Rules

Band setupAnchor a strong band or strap around a solid rack post at knee height. Step inside the loop so the band sits behind both knees. Walk back until there is firm band tension pulling the knees forward.
PositionStand with feet about shoulder-width apart. Sit back into a squat while keeping the torso fairly upright. The band supports the knees and allows the shins to stay more vertical.
ExecutionHold a comfortable squat depth. You should feel the quadriceps working. Keep heels down and knees tracking in line with toes. Start shallow if needed and do not chase depth.
Stop ruleThe knee should feel loaded but not sharp or irritated. Stop if pain rises above 3/10 or swelling is worse the next day.
AlternativeIf no band or strap is available, use a wall sit for 2 holds of 20-30 seconds. Keep the back against the wall, feet forward, and use a comfortable knee angle.
4-Week Progression

Build Capacity Without Testing It

WeekFocusProgression Rule
Week 1 - RestartUse 2 sets for most exercises. Keep everything easy. Prioritise movement quality.No soreness hunting.
Week 2 - RepeatKeep loads similar.Add a third set only where the previous week felt completely comfortable. Do not increase everything at once.
Week 3 - Small loadingAdd 2.5-5 kg to barbell lifts only if symptoms have stayed quiet.No increase in knee swelling, foot swelling, shoulder pain, elbow pain, limping, or karate-related soreness. Keep pressing conservative.

Green

Continue

  • Mild stiffness that improves during warm-up.
  • Normal muscle soreness.
  • Symmetrical tiredness.
  • No increase in swelling.
Amber

Reduce Load, Range, or Volume

  • Knee discomfort above 3/10.
  • Shoulder pinch.
  • Elbow pain with gripping or pressing.
  • Foot or ankle soreness that changes movement.
  • Next-day soreness that affects walking, stairs, or karate.
Red

Stop That Movement and Reassess

  • Sharp pain, giving way, limping, night pain, or new/worsening swelling.
  • Numbness, weakness, or neurological symptoms.
  • Pain that worsens as the session continues.
Avoid for 4 Weeks

Keep the Ceiling Low

  • Heavy squats or deadlifts.
  • Max-effort bench or overhead press.
  • HIIT, sprinting, plyometrics, or hard hill work.
  • Long barefoot walks.
  • High-volume push-ups or extra high-volume kicking drills outside karate.
  • Hard training the morning after a difficult karate session.
Fallback

Minimum Viable 20-Minute Session

  • 5-minute warm-up.
  • Goblet squat - 2 x 8.
  • Dumbbell floor press - 2 x 8.
  • One-arm row - 2 x 10 each side.
  • Romanian deadlift - 2 x 8.
  • Calf raises - 2 x 12.
Use this on busy or tired days instead of skipping completely. Keep it easy and leave the gym feeling better than when you started.
