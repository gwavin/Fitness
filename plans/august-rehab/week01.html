Week 1 Movement Library
How to use this page
  • Each day lists your exercises with sets & reps. Click any exercise to open the full how‑to, cues, mistakes, and regressions/progressions.
  • Pain rule: 0–3/10 is acceptable. Stop for sharp/catching pain or if soreness >24h next day. When in doubt, regress one step.
  • Priority order: 1) Shoulder scapula/cuff → 2) Elbow tendon protocol → 3) Strength → 4) Conditioning/Run.
Light & Easy – Week 1 Defaults
  • Weights days: use 2 sets of 6–8 reps at RPE 5–6 (easy). Stop with 2–3 reps in reserve and rest ~60–90 s.
  • Elbow isometrics: 20–30 s × 3 holds at 4–5/10 effort. If the next day feels great, you can add a 4th hold.
  • Grip: neutral grips where possible; straps allowed on hinge work if elbow talks.
  • Running: Running: keep it conversational, quiet feet. If next‑morning calf/Achilles stiffness > 60 min, repeat the same volume.

Week 1 – Alternating Days (Weights ↔ Run) + Daily Shoulder Rehab

Goal: settle elbow tendons (isometrics), establish scapula control daily, begin lower‑body strength, and start a careful barefoot run transition. Each day below is a single table of clickable items.

Monday — Weights + Elbow Care (~35 min)

Pendulum Swings — 30s each direction
  • Lean forward, hand on a table/chair. Let the affected arm hang completely relaxed.
  • Start tiny circles from your body momentum, not the shoulder muscles. Breathe slowly.
  • Do clockwise/counter‑clockwise, then gentle forward/back and side/side if comfortable.
Open Books (T‑spine) — 6 reps/side
  • Lie on your side, hips/knees at 90°. Arms straight in front, palms together.
  • Reach top hand to the ceiling, then rotate it open toward the floor behind you.
  • Keep knees touching and stacked; let chest/ribs rotate. Breathe out into the stretch.
  • Return with control. Stop short of any pinching.
Doorway Pec Stretch (optional; safe‑angle) — 20–30s ×1
  • Arm angle: set elbow below shoulder (≈45–60°), not at/above 90°.
  • Single‑arm setup: forearm on frame; take a small split stance.
  • Scapula set: gently draw shoulder blade "into the back pocket" (down/back) before leaning.
  • Ribcage rotation: rotate chest slightly through the doorway rather than cranking the arm backward.
  • External rotation cue: think “show the elbow pit forward” to keep the humeral head centered.
  • Intensity mild (2–3/10). Start 20s holds; build to 30s only if clean (no pinch).
  • Not OK: sharp front‑of‑shoulder pinch, tingling/numbness, or pain that lingers/worsens.

Alternative: Pec Minor Corner Stretch (low arm) — forearm on wall at ~45°, step and rotate chest away until a gentle stretch. Or use a lacrosse ball on pec minor (60–90s, light pressure).

Goblet Squat — 2×6–8 @ RPE 5–6 • 3‑1‑1 tempo
  • Hold KB/DB at chest; feet shoulder‑width; brace; sit between hips; chest tall; knees over mid‑foot.
  • 3s down • 1s pause • drive up over mid‑foot. Heels stay down.
Romanian Deadlift (DB/KB) — 2×6–8 very light–light
  • Soft knees; hinge by pushing hips back; spine neutral; lats packed.
  • Weights skim thighs; stop mid‑shin; 2s up. Use straps if elbow/grip protests.
Wrist Extensor Isometric — 20–30s ×3 @ 4–5/10 effort
  • Forearm supported, palm down. Hold light DB; use the other hand to gently push down; meet the pressure without pain.
Triceps Isometric (wall) — 15–20s ×3
  • Elbow 60–90°. Palm to wall. Press as if straightening the elbow. Keep shoulder down/back.
Scapular Setting — 5s ×10 (daily)
  • Gentle shrug → roll back → set blades down into back pockets; neck relaxed; breathe.
  • No pinching between blades; think width across collarbones.
Band Internal Rotation — 2×12–15 (very light, 3s return)
  • Anchor at elbow height; elbow glued to side at 90°.
  • Pull forearm toward belly; pause; slowly return 3s. Chest tall; ribs down.
  • Elbow drifting away; shoulder rolling forward.
Wall Angels — 2×8
  • Back/head/butt to wall; ribs gently down.
  • Slide arms up only to the point before winging or back arch; smooth breathing.

Regress: Seated or smaller range. Progress: Add 2–3s pause at top without shrug.

Push‑Up Plus (wall) — 1×12
  • Hands on wall; body straight. Perform push‑up; at the top, actively protract shoulder blades (the “plus”).
  • Neck long; no rib flare. Step feet back to increase difficulty.

Movement Library (Quick Reference)

All key moves with setup • execution • cues • common mistakes • regressions/progressions.

Pendulum Swings
  • Support yourself with the opposite hand on a table/chair. Let the working arm hang completely relaxed.
  • Use gentle body sway to create motion; the shoulder stays passive.
  • Small circles clockwise/counter‑clockwise 30–60s; then forward/back and side/side if comfortable.
  • Breathe slow; keep upper trap relaxed (no shrugging).
  • Not OK: sharp pain, catching, or actively lifting the arm.
Open Books (T‑spine)
  • Side‑lying with hips/knees 90°. Arms straight in front, palms together.
  • Reach top hand to ceiling, then rotate open toward the floor behind you.
  • Keep knees stacked and touching; allow ribs/chest to rotate; exhale into the stretch.
  • Return with control. Perform 6–8 slow reps/side.
  • Avoid pulling shoulder into pain; movement should feel like a chest/rib rotation, not a shoulder crank.
Doorway Pec Stretch
  • Arm angle: elbow below shoulder (≈45–60°), not at/above 90°.
  • Single‑arm setup: forearm on frame; take a small split stance.
  • Scapula set: gently draw shoulder blade "into the back pocket" (down/back) before leaning.
  • Ribcage rotation: rotate chest slightly through the doorway rather than cranking the arm backward.
  • External rotation cue: think “show the elbow pit forward” to keep humeral head centered.
  • Intensity mild (2–3/10). Start 20s holds; build to 30s ×2–3 only if no pinch.
  • Not OK: sharp anterior shoulder pinch, tingling/numbness, or lingering pain.

Alternative: Pec Minor Corner Stretch (arm at ~45°) or lacrosse‑ball pec minor release 60–90s.

Band External Rotation
  • Anchor band at elbow height; towel roll between elbow and ribs.
  • Elbow glued at 90°. Rotate forearm outward; pause; slow 3s return.
  • Think “show the elbow pit forward,” ribs down, neck long.
  • Avoid elbow drifting or upper‑trap shrug.
Band Internal Rotation
  • Anchor at elbow height; elbow at 90°, glued to side (use towel roll).
  • Rotate forearm toward belly; brief pause; return over 3s under control.
  • Chest tall; no shoulder roll‑forward.
  • Don’t let elbow drift or ribs flare.
Prone Y / T / W
  • Face‑down on bench or floor. Thumbs up.
  • Y: arms at ~120°. T: arms at 90°. W: elbows bent, squeeze blades to back pockets.
  • Small ROM; lift from lower/mid traps; keep neck neutral.
  • Avoid shrugging or craning neck.
Push‑Up Plus (wall/incline/floor)
  • Set a solid plank (ribs down, glutes lightly on). Lower and press up like a normal push‑up.
  • At the top, actively protract shoulder blades (the “plus”) without shrugging.
  • Progress wall → bench/incline → floor.
  • Avoid rib flare, head forward, or collapsing shoulders.
Pronation / Supination (DB hammer)
  • Elbow at side, 90° bend. Hold light DB like a hammer.
  • Rotate slowly palm‑down (pronate) then palm‑up (supinate) through pain‑free range.
  • Keep elbow glued to ribs; slow tempo.
  • Don’t overshoot into painful end ranges.
Short‑Foot Drill
  • While standing, gently pull ball of big toe toward heel to lift the arch.
  • Toes stay relaxed; hold 10–20s; breathe; avoid cramping.
  • Use barefoot shoes or socks for best feedback.
Calf Raises (DL / SL)
  • Rise over big toe/second toe; pause at top 1s; lower slowly 2–3s.
  • DL: both legs; SL: one leg supported by fingertip balance if needed.
  • Full range without bouncing; keep ankles from rolling out.
Tibialis Raises
  • Back against wall, heels ~15–20 cm away. Lift toes toward shins high and slow.
  • Lower under control; feel along the front of the shin.
Chest‑Supported Row (Neutral)
  • Lying prone on an incline bench, neutral‑grip DBs.
  • Pull elbows toward hips; squeeze shoulder blades down/back; keep neck long.
  • Lower under control; no shrugging.
Neutral‑Grip DB Press (Incline)
  • Set slight incline; neutral grips; elbows ~30–45° from torso.
  • Press smoothly without shoulder pain; stop short of discomfort.
  • Keep scapulae lightly retracted/depressed (no shrug).
Suitcase / Farmer’s Carry
  • Stand tall; ribs down; shoulders level; walk at a steady pace.
  • Suitcase: one side at a time; resist leaning. Farmer’s: both sides loaded.
  • Choose loads that don’t provoke elbow; swap to isometric holds if needed.

Safety: If sharp pain, catching, or progressive weakness develops, pause and seek in‑person assessment. Progress only when next‑day symptoms ≤3/10. Keep shoulder blades back+down; keep elbow tucked when possible; use neutral grips early.
