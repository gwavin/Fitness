Clinical focus

Restore tolerance, not heroics. Symptoms, gait quality and shoulder control guide progression.

Run priority

Cadence 170 to 180 steps per minute with quiet midfoot contact and tall posture.

Strength priority

Rebuild barbell rhythm with clean technical reps before chasing previous numbers.

Progression gate

Advance only if symptoms settle quickly and the next day feels normal.

Training Philosophy

This block should feel calm, clinical and repeatable. The goal is to finish each session with enough quality left in the tank to repeat it 48 hours later.

  • Use the diagnostic week first. Confirm the shoulder, feet, calves and Achilles respond well before volume rises.
  • Keep the run relaxed. Light ground contact matters more than pace or distance.
  • Lift submaximally. Week 1 to 2 strength work should leave obvious reps in reserve.
  • Progress with symptom rules. Pain above 2 out of 10 or next-day flare-up means repeat, not advance.
Simple rule: If cadence deteriorates, landing becomes noisy, or pressing feels unstable, stay on the same week and reduce ambition before you increase load.

Goblet squat diagnostic

Load: 20 kg dumbbell.
Dose: 3 sets of 10 with stable heels, even depth and braced posture.

Shoulder diagnostic

Sequence: 10 push-ups, 10 inverted rows, 20-second dead hang. Stop if pain sharpens or stability drops.

Week 0 decision point: If the shoulder feels stable and calf or foot soreness settles by the next day, progress to Week 1. If not, repeat Week 0 once before extending the running intervals.

Scheduling Notes

Run first, lift second. The run remains the main tissue exposure for the lower leg and foot.

Protect recovery days. Tuesday and Thursday are there to help the program work, not because training is missing.

Keep Saturday flexible. If the lower legs feel heavy, take the full rest option.

Safety Guidance

Use a traffic-light system to decide whether to advance. Conservative decisions early in the block usually create better momentum later.

Green light

Pain 0 to 2 out of 10, no limp, no slappy landing, no shoulder instability, and morning stiffness settles by the next day. Continue.

Amber light

Symptoms are mild but noticeable, or soreness lasts into the next day. Repeat the same week and reduce load slightly.

Red light

Sharp pain, altered gait, marked Achilles pain, swelling or shoulder instability. Stop and seek clinical review before progressing.

Running stop signs: forefoot burning, calf tightening that changes cadence, or the need to reach forward with the foot to keep pace.
Lifting stop signs: shoulder pinching on pressing, loss of scapular control, or repeated grindy reps that alter movement quality.
