Week 5 – Push–Pull & Shoulder Care

Balance pushing and pulling exercises while reinforcing shoulder stability.

Warm‑Up

Begin with 5–7 minutes of low‑impact activity like brisk walking or cycling followed by dynamic stretches for the shoulders and hips (arm circles, leg swings). Warming up increases circulation and prepares the muscles for exercise; research from the American Academy of Orthopaedic Surgeons suggests a 5–10‑minute low‑impact warm up before strengthening exercises and stretching afterwards【963587572219979†L210-L217】.

Workout (≈35 minutes)

  • Dumbbell Bench Press: 3 sets of 10 reps. Keep wrists stacked and elbows at 45°.
  • Bent‑Over Row: 3 sets of 10 reps. Squeeze shoulder blades at the top.
  • External Rotation & Band Pull‑Apart: 3 sets of 12 reps each. Perform external rotations then band pull‑aparts back‑to‑back.
  • Push‑Up Variation: 3 sets of 12 reps (kneeling or incline if needed). Maintain a rigid plank and lower chest to just above the floor.
  • Dead Bug: 3 sets of 10 reps per side. Keep lower back pressed into the floor.

Cooldown

Finish with about 5 minutes of gentle stretching (hamstrings, quadriceps, chest, and shoulders) to restore range of motion and reduce muscle soreness【963587572219979†L210-L217】.
