Week 5 – Conditioning & Core Circuit

Challenge your cardiovascular system and core with a kettlebell‑based circuit.

Warm‑Up

Begin with 5–7 minutes of low‑impact activity like brisk walking or cycling followed by dynamic stretches for the shoulders and hips (arm circles, leg swings). Warming up increases circulation and prepares the muscles for exercise; research from the American Academy of Orthopaedic Surgeons suggests a 5–10‑minute low‑impact warm up before strengthening exercises and stretching afterwards【963587572219979†L210-L217】.

Workout (≈35 minutes)

  • Kettlebell Deadlift to High Pull: 3 sets of 12 reps. Perform a deadlift then drive the kettlebell up to chest height.
  • Goblet Squat to Press: 3 sets of 10 reps. Squat then press the weight overhead in one smooth motion.
  • Farmer’s Carry: 3 sets of 30‑second carries with dumbbells or kettlebells.
  • Plank with Knee Tuck: 3 sets of 12 reps using sliders or towels under your feet.
  • Mountain Climber: 3 sets of 20 reps per side. Keep your hips low and move quickly.

Cooldown

Finish with about 5 minutes of gentle stretching (hamstrings, quadriceps, chest, and shoulders) to restore range of motion and reduce muscle soreness【963587572219979†L210-L217】.
