Kihon Ippon Kumite – Full Directions 27102025

Overview: “Kihon Ippon Kumite” is the basic one‑step sparring drill in Shotokan karate. One person delivers a predefined attack, and the partner counters with a block and a single decisive counter‑attack. Practising from both sides builds correct stances, breathing, etiquette and awareness. The techniques below are split into units for easier study, and colours inspired by Shotokan (reds and earthy tones) highlight each section.

Sidedness Note: In Shotokan karate, all techniques in Kihon Ippon Kumite are practised from both sides (right side lead; left side lead) to develop balance and symmetry. The descriptions below assume a right‑lead forward stance for the attacker (stepping back with the left foot to assume stance, then advancing with the right foot). For the left side, mirror all actions (step back with right foot, etc.). Partners switch roles after each repetition, and always bow before and after exchanges. Emphasise focus at the end of each technique, with proper awareness maintained throughout. Specific left/right details for arms and feet are noted inline for clarity.

A. High‑Level Lunge Punches (Jōdan Oi‑Zuki)

1. “Up‑and‑Over” – Jōdan Oi‑Zuki #1

  • Attacker: Begin in the ready stance (yoi). Step your left foot straight back into a long forward stance (right zenkutsu‑dachi) while performing a downward sweeping block with the right lead arm (gedan barai). Immediately drive forward with the right foot, shifting your weight to the right front leg and delivering a high lunge punch (jōdan oi‑zuki) with the right arm aimed at the opponent’s head. After the punch lands, withdraw by pulling the right front leg back and returning to the natural ready stance.
  • Defender:
    • Stay relaxed in the ready stance until the attack begins.
    • As the punch approaches, lift the right front arm into a rising block (age‑uke) to shield the head.
    • Immediately counter by twisting the hips and punching with the left rear hand to the opponent’s mid‑section (chūdan gyaku‑zuki).
    • Hold the finishing posture briefly, then return to yoi.

2. “Knife‑Hand Intercept” – Jōdan Oi‑Zuki #2

  • Attacker: Start from yoi. Step left foot back into a forward stance (right zenkutsu‑dachi), sweeping the right front arm down in a downward block. Immediately shift your body weight forward with the right foot and punch to the opponent’s head with a high lunge punch (jōdan oi‑zuki) using the right arm. After impact, bring the right front leg back and return to the ready position.
  • Defender:
    • Remain in the ready stance.
    • As the punch comes in, rotate your torso to the left and place both hands vertical with the right lead hand acting as a knife‑hand block (tate shuto‑uke) while shifting the left foot back into back stance (kokutsu‑dachi).
    • Immediately counter with the left rear hand by executing a high knife‑hand strike (gyaku jōdan shuto‑uchi) to the attacker’s neck.
    • Recover by bringing the left back leg forward to yoi.

3. “Side‑Kick Surprise” – Jōdan Oi‑Zuki #3

  • Attacker: From the ready stance, step left foot back into a forward stance (right zenkutsu‑dachi) with a downward sweep (gedan barai) using the right arm. Immediately thrust forward with the right foot, shifting your centre and performing a high lunge punch towards the head with the right arm. After striking, pull the right punching arm back and return your right front leg to the ready stance.
  • Defender:
    • Maintain yoi until the attack initiates.
    • As the punch nears, step to the left side with the left foot into a slightly closed stance and perform a side rising block (sokumen jōdan‑uke) with the right arm to deflect the punch.
    • Without hesitation, lift the right leg (closest to the opponent) and execute a snapping side kick (yoko‑geri keage) to the attacker’s ribs.
    • Land in horse stance (kiba‑dachi) and finish with a right side elbow strike (yoko enpi‑uchi) before returning to yoi.

4. “Roundhouse Elbow Clinch” – Jōdan Oi‑Zuki #4

  • Attacker: Begin in yoi. Step left foot back with the lead foot into a long forward stance (right zenkutsu‑dachi) while executing a downward sweep with the right arm. Immediately lunge forward with the right foot and punch to the head with a high lunge punch using the right arm. After the strike, retract the right front leg to return to yoi.
  • Defender:
    • As the attacker moves forward, step back with the left foot into forward stance (right zenkutsu‑dachi) and cross your wrists (right over left) to form a back‑hand X‑block (haishu juji‑uke).
    • Counter with a left roundhouse kick (mawashi‑geri) using the left rear leg to the attacker’s ribs.
    • Grab their right wrist (te zukami) with your left hand to control their arm.
    • Then pivot on the right foot and deliver a left roundhouse elbow strike (mawashi enpi‑uchi) with the left arm.
    • Release and return to yoi.

B. Mid‑Level Lunge Punches (Chūdan Oi‑Zuki)

1. “Outer Block & Lunge” – Chūdan Oi‑Zuki #1

  • Attacker: Starting from yoi, step left foot back into a forward stance (right zenkutsu‑dachi) and sweep the right lead hand downward (gedan barai). Immediately shift the body forward with the right foot and punch to the mid‑section (chūdan oi‑zuki) with the right arm. Retract by bringing the right front leg back to yoi.
  • Defender:
    • Remain in yoi.
    • As the punch arrives, step back with the left foot into a forward stance (right zenkutsu‑dachi) and deflect it using an outside fore‑arm block (soto‑ude‑uke) with the left arm.
    • Immediately counter with a middle oi‑zuki with the right front arm to the attacker’s solar plexus.
    • Then return to ready.

2. “Horse‑Stance Elbow” – Chūdan Oi‑Zuki #2

  • Attacker: From the ready position, step left foot back into a forward stance (right zenkutsu‑dachi) while sweeping down with the right lead arm. Immediately thrust forward with the right foot and punch to the opponent’s mid‑section (chūdan oi‑zuki) with the right arm, then bring the right front leg back to yoi.
  • Defender:
    • Step back with the left foot into a forward stance (right zenkutsu‑dachi) and perform an outside fore‑arm block (soto‑ude‑uke) with the right arm.
    • Slide both feet forward (yori‑ashi) into horse stance (kiba‑dachi).
    • Twist the hips to the left and strike with a right side elbow (yoko enpi‑uchi) to the attacker’s ribs.
    • Recover to yoi.

3. “Inside‑Jab Reverse” – Chūdan Oi‑Zuki #3

  • Attacker: From yoi, step left foot back with the lead foot into forward stance (right zenkutsu‑dachi) and sweep down with a downward block using the right arm. Immediately drive forward with the right foot and punch to the opponent’s mid‑section (chūdan oi‑zuki) with the right arm. After punching, pull the right front leg back to return to the ready stance.
  • Defender:
    • As the attacker steps, step back with the left foot into forward stance (right zenkutsu‑dachi) and perform an inside fore‑arm block (uchi‑ude‑uke) with the left arm.
    • With no pause, jab with the right front hand to the opponent’s face (jōdan kizami‑zuki).
    • Then drive a middle reverse punch with the left rear hand.
    • Resume the ready stance.

4. “Knife‑Hand Spear” – Chūdan Oi‑Zuki #4

  • Attacker: Begin in yoi. Step left foot back, sweep down with the right lead arm and lunge forward with the right foot with a punch to the mid‑section using the right arm. After punching, retreat by pulling the right front leg back to yoi.
  • Defender:
    • Pivot 45° to the left with the right foot into back stance (kokutsu‑dachi) and perform a knife‑hand block (shuto‑uke) with the right arm.
    • Snap a front kick (kizami mae‑geri) with the right front leg to the attacker’s abdomen.
    • Then thrust your left rear fingers forward in a reverse spear‑hand (gyaku yōhan nukite) aimed at the throat.
    • Recover to the ready stance.

5. “Pivot & Rear Elbow” – Chūdan Oi‑Zuki #5

  • Attacker: From the ready stance, step left foot back into forward stance (right zenkutsu‑dachi) with a downward sweep using the right arm. Immediately advance with the right foot and punch to the mid‑section (chūdan oi‑zuki) with the right arm, then pull the right front leg back to the ready position.
  • Defender:
    • Move into horse stance (kiba‑dachi) and execute an elbow block (enpi‑uke) with the right arm.
    • Perform a tenshin (pivot) by turning on the ball of the right front foot, allowing your body to rotate 180° to the left.
    • From this turned position, deliver a rear roundhouse elbow strike (ushiro mawashi enpi‑uchi) with the left arm toward the attacker.
    • Finish by stepping back to yoi.

C. Front‑Kick Counters (Mae‑Geri)

1. “Low Sweep & Punch” – Mae‑Geri #1

  • Attacker: From yoi, step left foot back into forward stance (right zenkutsu‑dachi) with both fists lowered (ryōwan gedan gamae). Kick forward with the left rear leg using a mid‑level front kick (chūdan mae‑geri) and land into forward stance with the left foot forward. After the kick, return the left front leg to yoi.
  • Defender:
    • Remain in natural stance.
    • As the kick comes, step back with the left foot into forward stance (right zenkutsu‑dachi) and sweep the kicking leg aside using a downward sweeping block (gedan barai) with the right arm.
    • Immediately punch the attacker’s mid‑section with a reverse punch (chūdan gyaku‑zuki) using the left arm.
    • Return to yoi.

2. “Reverse Sweep Combo” – Mae‑Geri #2

  • Attacker: Adopt the ready position. Step left foot back with the lead foot into forward stance (right zenkutsu‑dachi) and lower both fists. Deliver a mid‑level front kick (chūdan mae‑geri) with the left rear leg, placing the left foot forward. After the kick, pull the left front leg back and return to yoi.
  • Defender:
    • As the kick approaches, step back with the left foot and perform a reverse downward sweep (gyaku gedan barai) with the left arm, moving into forward stance (right zenkutsu‑dachi).
    • Counter with a jabbing punch to the face (jōdan kizami‑zuki) with the right arm.
    • Then twist the hips and deliver a reverse punch to the torso (gyaku‑zuki) with the left arm.
    • Finish by stepping back to the ready stance.

3. “Cross‑Block Smash” – Mae‑Geri #3

  • Attacker: Stand in yoi. Step left foot back into a forward stance (right zenkutsu‑dachi) with both fists lowered. Fire a mid‑level front kick (chūdan mae‑geri) with the left rear leg toward the defender’s abdomen. Replace the left foot forward in forward stance and then bring it back to return to the ready position.
  • Defender:
    • As the attack begins, draw the right front foot back into a long front stance (left zenkutsu‑dachi), crossing your forearms (right over left) into a low X‑block (gedan juji‑uke) to catch and redirect the kick.
    • Immediately twist the hips forward and strike the attacker’s head with a double knife‑hand strike (jōdan shuto juji uchi) using both hands.
    • Keep the feet planted in the stance during the strike.
    • Then step forward with the right rear leg to return to yoi.
  • Footwork note: Stepping the front foot back lengthens your stance and loads the rear leg. After blocking and striking, bring the rear foot forward to reset.

4. “Cat‑Stance Elbow” – Mae‑Geri #4

  • Attacker: From yoi, step left foot back into forward stance (right zenkutsu‑dachi) with hands low, then kick forward with the left rear leg using a mid‑level front kick (chūdan mae‑geri). Land forward with the left foot, then return the left front leg to yoi.
  • Defender:
    • Slide your body to the left side with the left foot into a cat stance (neko‑ashi‑dachi), and use a low sweeping block (gedan barai) with the right arm to deflect the kick.
    • Immediately execute a reverse vertical knife‑hand strike (gyaku tate shuto‑uchi) to the attacker's neck.
    • Land in horse stance (kiba‑dachi) and deliver a right elbow strike (age enpi‑uchi) to the face.
    • Return to yoi.

D. Side‑Kick Counters (Chūdan Yoko‑Geri)

The attacker steps back with their left foot into a right forward stance (zenkutsu‑dachi) with guard (kamae). They then execute a mid‑level side kick (chūdan yoko‑geri) with the left rear leg, landing forward before returning to yoi.

1. “Outside Block & Ridge‑Hand” – Yoko‑Geri #1

  • Attacker: From yoi, step left foot back into forward stance (right zenkutsu‑dachi) with fists in guard (kamae). Execute a mid‑level side kick (chūdan yoko‑geri) with the left rear leg. Land forward with the left foot, then return to yoi.
  • Defender:
    • As the kick approaches, step back with the left foot into a right forward stance (right zenkutsu‑dachi).
    • Perform an outside forearm block (soto‑ude‑uke) with the left arm to deflect the kicking leg.
    • Immediately counter by sliding in (yori‑ashi) and striking with a right ridge‑hand (haito‑uchi) to the attacker's neck or floating ribs.
    • Return to yoi.

2. “Evasion & Backfist” – Yoko‑Geri #2

  • Attacker: From yoi, step left foot back into forward stance (right zenkutsu‑dachi) with fists in guard (kamae). Execute a mid‑level side kick (chūdan yoko‑geri) with the left rear leg. Land forward with the left foot, then return to yoi.
  • Defender:
    • As the kick begins, pivot 45 degrees to the left on your right foot (tai‑sabaki), moving your body offline from the attack.
    • Let the kick pass in front of you.
    • As the attacker's leg retracts, immediately step in with your left foot and strike with a right backfist (uraken‑uchi) to the side of their head.
    • Return to yoi.

E. Roundhouse‑Kick Counters (Chūdan Mawashi‑Geri)

The attacker steps back with their left foot into a right forward stance (zenkutsu‑dachi) with guard (kamae). They then execute a mid‑level roundhouse kick (chūdan mawashi‑geri) with the left rear leg, landing forward before returning to yoi.

1. “Inside Block & Reverse Punch” – Mawashi‑Geri #1

  • Attacker: From yoi, step left foot back into forward stance (right zenkutsu‑dachi) with fists in guard (kamae). Execute a mid‑level roundhouse kick (chūdan mawashi‑geri) with the left rear leg. Land forward with the left foot, then return to yoi.
  • Defender:
    • As the kick comes, step back with the left foot into a right forward stance (right zenkutsu‑dachi).
    • Block the kick with a strong left inside forearm block (uchi‑ude‑uke), aiming for the attacker's shin.
    • Immediately counter with a right reverse punch (chūdan gyaku‑zuki) to the solar plexus.
    • Return to yoi.

2. “Leg Check & Elbow Strike” – Mawashi‑Geri #2

  • Attacker: From yoi, step left foot back into forward stance (right zenkutsu‑dachi) with fists in guard (kamae). Execute a mid‑level roundhouse kick (chūdan mawashi‑geri) with the left rear leg. Land forward with the left foot, then return to yoi.
  • Defender:
    • Instead of stepping back, lift your left lead leg to perform a “leg check” (sune‑uke), blocking the kick with your shin.
    • Place your left foot down forward into a left forward stance (left zenkutsu‑dachi).
    • Simultaneously, deliver a right rising elbow strike (age enpi‑uchi) under the attacker's chin.
    • Return to yoi.

3. “Capture, Sweep & Punch” – Mawashi‑Geri #3

  • Attacker: From yoi, step left foot back into forward stance (right zenkutsu‑dachi) with fists in guard (kamae). Execute a mid‑level roundhouse kick (chūdan mawashi‑geri) with the left rear leg. Land forward with the left foot, then return to yoi.
  • Defender:
    • Step back with the left foot (right zenkutsu‑dachi) and catch the kicking leg with a low X‑block (gedan juji‑uke).
    • Secure the attacker's leg by wrapping your left arm around it.
    • Execute a right front sweep (de‑ashi‑barai) to their supporting (right) leg while pulling their captured leg.
    • [In practice, just show the unbalancing, do not complete the takedown.]
    • Finish with a right reverse punch (gyaku‑zuki) as they fall. Release and return to yoi.
