Kumite Techniques & Session Builder

Use this guide to move from warm-up to live kumite with intention. Each block highlights the technical focus, key cues, and simple volume targets so you can scale intensity for solo, pad or partner practice. Pair it with the Karate Warm-Up Flow to keep sessions joint-friendly.

Session structure

  • Prime & breathe
  • Footwork foundations
  • Angles & entries
  • Core striking combinations
  • Defence & counters
  • Partner reaction drills
  • Conditioning finisher
  • Weekly emphasis planner
  • Cool-down & reflection

Prime & breathe (10 minutes)

Flow directly from the warm-up sequence into light shadowboxing. Focus on nasal breathing, loose shoulders and clean hip rotation. Finish with 2 × 60 seconds of easy jab–reverse punch shadow rounds to set rhythm.

Footwork foundations

  • Guard settling: 3 × 30 seconds stance holds, check even pressure through the feet.
  • Forward/back slides: 3 × 20 steps keeping head level, snap back to guard.
  • Lateral shuffles: 4 × 15 seconds per side, finish with a sharp half-step.
  • Triangle steps: 3 × 40 seconds, emphasise leading with the hips not the shoulders.

Angles & entries

  • V-step with jab: 3 × 10 reps, push off rear leg and recover with small shuffle.
  • Switch-step burst: 3 × 6 reps, add gyaku-zuki as you land in orthodox.
  • Pivot exit drill: 3 × 8 reps, rotate on lead foot after combination, eyes up.
  • Feint & draw: 2 × 1 minute, light feints to bait a response before entering.

Core striking combinations

  • Oi-zuki repetition: 4 × 6 reps each side, drive from hips, exhale on impact.
  • Gyaku-zuki focus: 4 × 8 reps, retract quickly, check guard every time.
  • Mae-geri mechanics: 3 × 6 per leg, snap the kick then control the chamber.
  • Jab → gyaku-zuki → mawashi-geri: 3 × 8 sequences, land balanced.
  • Lead hook kick setups: 3 × 6 sequences using feint jab to create space.

Defence & counters

  • High guard parry: 3 × 10 reps, redirect straight punch with minimal movement.
  • Slip outside reverse punch: 3 × 8 reps each side, counter with gyaku-zuki.
  • Mae-geri check: 3 × 6 per leg, catch with rear hand and step offline.
  • Cover & angle out: 3 × 5 sequences, shield then pivot to open counter line.

Partner reaction drills

  • Call & respond: 3 × 90 seconds, partner calls technique and you execute instantly.
  • Tag sparring: 3 × 2 minutes at 40% intensity, focus on clean touches not power.
  • Counter for counter: 4 × 45 seconds, trade controlled counters with preset pattern.
  • Scenario start: 3 × 1 minute from clinch or corner positions to practice resets.

Conditioning finisher

  • 30 seconds: Fast jab–cross shadowboxing.
  • 30 seconds: Alternating mae-geri with guard recovery.
  • 30 seconds: Sprawl to fighting stance.
  • Rest 30 seconds and repeat the circuit 3–4 times based on energy.

Weekly emphasis planner

Session Primary focus Key cues
Day 1 Footwork mechanics Stay relaxed, rehearse entries at 70% speed before adding strikes.
Day 2 Combination polish Record 2 rounds of pad work, review hip rotation and guard recovery.
Day 3 Defence & reaction Alternate rounds of tag sparring and counter-for-counter work.

Cool-down & reflection

Close the session with 3 minutes of slow shadowboxing, then revisit two static stretches from the warm-up to reset posture. Capture quick notes:

  • What felt sharp or sluggish today?
  • Which entries landed cleanly?
  • One adjustment for your next session.
