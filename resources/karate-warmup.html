Karate Warm-Up Flow

This dynamic-to-static warm-up primes major joints, engages posture muscles and sets a calm breathing cadence before kumite or kata work. Run through the two phases back-to-back, adjusting the timing to suit your training partners.

Dynamic Activation (5–7 minutes)

  • Neck Rotations: Gentle circles both directions, synchronise with breathing.
  • Shoulder Rotations: Forward and backward rolls to free the scapula.
  • Arm Swings: Large circles and cross-body swings, gradually increasing range.
  • Torso Twists: Feet shoulder-width, rotate hips and shoulders together.
  • Hip Rotations: Slow figure-eight patterns to unlock the pelvis.
  • Leg Swings: Forward/back and lateral swings, light guard up for balance.
  • High Knees: Tall posture, land softly to wake up footwork rhythm.
  • Butt Kicks: Maintain cadence, think fast recovery between strikes.

Targeted Mobility (3–5 minutes)

  • Hamstring Fold: Seat tall, hinge forward, hold 20–30 seconds each side.
  • Quad Stretch: Stand tall, grab ankle, keep knees together for 20–30 seconds.
  • Groin Opener: Butterfly position, gentle press of knees toward floor.
  • Calf Lean: Staggered stance into wall, back heel grounded, swap sides.
Layer it with kumite practice. After mobility work, flow straight into kumite technique rounds or kata basics while your nervous system is primed. Finish with relaxed shadowboxing to consolidate the movement pattern.
