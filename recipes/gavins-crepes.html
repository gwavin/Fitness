Gavin’s Crêpes (The Golden Ticket)

Written after a sequence of emails — now in tidy recipe form.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup wholemeal flour
  • 1 cup white flour
  • 2⅓ cups milk
  • 3 eggs
  • ~⅓ cup butter, melted
  • ½ tsp salt
Best combo: melted cheddar + salmon.
(Add anything else you like after the cheese melts.)
Resting matters: blend the batter and leave it to settle for 20 minutes.

Gear & setup

  • Flat pan
  • Extractor fan on + window open
  • Biggest burner set to about “7pm” (your knob’s marking)
Ventilation note: butter + hot pan can smoke — fan on and window open is the move.
Quick test: “glob” about 10 mL batter onto the hot pan.
When it browns, you’re flying.

Method

  1. Blend the batter: wholemeal flour + white flour + milk + eggs + melted butter + salt.
  2. Rest: leave the batter to settle for 20 minutes.
  3. Heat the pan: flat pan on the biggest burner, set around “7pm”. Fan already on, window open.
  4. Test the heat: glob ~10 mL batter onto the pan. If it browns nicely, you’re ready.
  5. Cook first side: pour batter for a crêpe and let the first side go until golden brown. (Golden brown is the ticket.)
  6. Flip: flip or lift-and-turn.
  7. Add cheddar immediately: pop the cheddar straight on after flipping.
  8. After the cheddar has melted: add the other ingredients (e.g., salmon).
  9. Finish: turn once, maybe once more, then plate up.
Doneness cue: lift an edge; if it’s properly golden, it’s ready to flip.
Optional tweaks (if you want to refine later)
  • For thinner crêpes: splash in a bit more milk until it pours like single cream.
  • For crispier edges: slightly hotter pan, slightly less batter.
  • For sweeter crêpes: add 1–2 tsp sugar (but this recipe is savoury-leaning).
