Week 1 – Upper Body & Shoulder Rehab

Focus on gentle pressing and rowing movements while reinforcing rotator cuff stability.

Warm‑Up

Begin with 5–7 minutes of low‑impact activity like brisk walking or cycling followed by dynamic stretches for the shoulders and hips (arm circles, leg swings). Warming up increases circulation and prepares the muscles for exercise; research from the American Academy of Orthopaedic Surgeons suggests a 5–10‑minute low‑impact warm up before strengthening exercises and stretching afterwards【963587572219979†L210-L217】.

Workout (≈35 minutes)

  • Incline Dumbbell Press: 3 sets of 12 reps using a light weight. Keep elbows at 45° to torso for shoulder comfort.
  • Bent‑Over Row: 3 sets of 10 reps with barbell or dumbbells. Pull elbows back and squeeze shoulder blades.
  • Scapular Pull‑Apart: 3 sets of 15 reps using a resistance band or light dumbbells. Keep arms straight and squeeze the mid‑back.
  • External Rotation: 3 sets of 12 reps per arm using a light dumbbell or band. Keep elbow tucked at your side while rotating your forearm out.
  • Side Plank: 2 sets of 30‑second holds per side to strengthen obliques and shoulder stabilisers.

Cooldown

Finish with about 5 minutes of gentle stretching (hamstrings, quadriceps, chest, and shoulders) to restore range of motion and reduce muscle soreness【963587572219979†L210-L217】.
