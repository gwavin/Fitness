Program Notes & Rehab Guidance

  • Equipment: barbell (rack), 20 kg kettlebell, 21 kg dumbbell, plus bodyweight only.
  • Shoulder Care: floor press at 50–60% 1RM, focus on rotator cuff work, limit horizontal press frequency.
  • Time Efficiency: group DB/KB exercises to reduce weight swaps; superset warm-up drills if short on time.
  • Progression Timeline: after 2–4 weeks of consistent, pain-free training, reassess:
    • Increase barbell lifts by ~5% or 2.5 kg if sessions feel controlled.
    • Add 1–2 reps to DB/KB movements (e.g., 3×12→3×14).
    • Reintroduce incline or bench press at 50–60% 1RM once weekly, monitoring pain.
  • Recovery: continue daily evening rehab; adjust volume if soreness increases.
  • Shoulder Rehab Tips:
    • Eccentric Emphasis: slow 3–4 s lowering on band rotations and YTWs to stimulate tendon remodeling.
    • Isometric Holds: add 10–15 s mid-range holds on external rotations and YTWs for static stability.
    • Monitor Volume: reduce pressing/rehab volume by ~20% for 3–5 days if pain flares, then resume.
    • Expected Timeline: improvements in 4–6 weeks; follow progression thereafter.

Monday – Lower Body & Press

  • Perform the Daily Warm-Up & Rotator Cuff routine above before lifting.
  • Back Squat (Barbell): 4×5 @ 70–75% 1RM – heavier compound focus.
  • Goblet Squat (20 kg KB): 2×12 – controlled descent (2 s eccentric).
  • Floor Press (21 kg DB): 5×5 @ 50–60% – maintain shoulder-friendly pressing.
  • One-Arm KB Row (20 kg KB): 3×10 each side – balance horizontal pull.
  • Plank (Bodyweight): 2×1 min – core stability.

Tuesday – Deadlift & Rows

  • Perform the Daily Warm-Up & Rotator Cuff routine above before lifting.
  • Deadlift (Barbell): 4×5 @ 70–75% 1RM.
  • Bent-Over Row (21 kg DB): 3×10 each side.
  • Single-Leg RDL (20 kg KB): 3×8 each side.
  • Rear Delt Raises (Bodyweight or 5–10 kg KB): 2×15
    Stand with feet hip-width apart and hinge at the hips until your torso is nearly parallel to the floor. Keep a slight bend in the knees and a neutral spine. With arms hanging down, raise both arms out to the sides until they reach shoulder height, leading with the elbows and squeezing the shoulder blades together. Lower slowly back to the start (3–4 s eccentric). Focus on scapular retraction rather than lifting heavy weight to maximize posterior shoulder activation. Demo

Wednesday – Arms & Shoulder Rehab

  • Perform the Daily Warm-Up & Rotator Cuff routine above before lifting.
  • Shrugs (20 kg KB): 3×12.
  • Hammer Curls (21 kg DB): 3×5
  • YTWs (Bodyweight): 2×10 each.
  • Band External Rotation (Light band): 2×15 each side.

Thursday – Squat Variation & Push

  • Perform the Daily Warm-Up & Rotator Cuff routine above before lifting.
  • Back Squat (Barbell): 4×5 @ 65–70% 1RM – if comfortable, maintains lower-body strength without shoulder load.
  • Alternative Goblet Squat (20 kg KB): 3×10 – use if front or back squat causes shoulder/back discomfort.
  • Push-Ups (Bodyweight): 3×10 – pain-free range only.
  • Shrugs (20 kg KB): 2×15 slow – scapular control.
  • Tip: Work on thoracic and shoulder mobility before reintroducing front squats; use goblet/back squat variation meanwhile.

Friday – Posterior Chain & Core

  • Perform the Daily Warm-Up & Rotator Cuff routine above before lifting.
  • Deadlift (Barbell, lighter): 4×5 @ 60–65%.
  • Bird-Dog Rows (Bodyweight): 3×10 each side.
  • Glute Bridge (Bodyweight): 3×12.
  • Plank (Bodyweight): 2×1 min.

Saturday – DB Medley & Conditioning

  • Perform the Daily Warm-Up & Rotator Cuff routine above before lifting.
  • DB Floor Press (21 kg DB): 3×10.
  • Renegade Rows (21 kg DB): 3×8 each side.
  • Lateral + Front Raises (Light DB/Kettlebell): 2–3×12–15 with a lighter load (5–10 kg).
    Use a weight you can control for full range without shoulder pain. If no lighter DB/Kettlebell, perform band or bodyweight T raises instead.
  • Farmer’s Carry (20 kg KB): 3 rounds of 30 s.
  • Bodyweight Squats: 2×15.

Sunday – Recovery & Rehab

  • Walk or Mobility Flow (Bodyweight): 20 min.
  • Rehab Circuit (Bodyweight): wall slides, shoulder rolls, YTWs.
