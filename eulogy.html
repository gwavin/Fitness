Eulogy for My Father

A loving husband, devoted grandfather, and steadfast friend.

Pause
Today we gather to remember my father — a loving husband, a devoted grandfather, and a man whose life touched more people than any short reflection could hope to capture.

Pause
In truth, his eulogy almost writes itself. Everyone I speak to has another story, another memory — enough to fill far more time than we have today. That alone tells you something important about him.

Pause
He was taken from us far too soon. At seventy-one, it feels as though more time was owed. And yet, in the years he was given, he lived fully, wringing what feels like many lifetimes out of the life he had.

Pause
He had a remarkable impact on everyone he met. He was a friend to all, always with a good word for everyone. But he offered more than words — he offered his hands and his time. Looking back, he performed an almost impossible number of good deeds for the people around him. If help was needed, he didn’t just offer; he turned up with tools and skill.

Pause
In his career as a guard, he carried responsibility with fairness and humanity. He was measured in his judgement, never acting rashly or harshly, and never approaching people with malice. He retired young, almost twenty years ago, but never stopped being useful, practical, or generous with his time.

Pause
Over years of working with colleagues and friends, he managed something rare: he moved through life without bitterness, without falling out, and without leaving problems behind him. To him, there was no problem that didn’t come with a solution.

Pause
He was a complete messer, always having the crack, always ready with a laugh or a story, and always lifting the mood wherever he was. He was a renowned storyteller, not just because he exaggerated, but because he noticed people and moments; he never missed a detail and knew how to share them.

Pause
He had many interests, but what stood out was the way he applied himself to them. Whether it was tug-of-war, golf, or welding — specifically trailers — he took real satisfaction in doing things properly and seeing them work. He believed in learning through doing, and he was always doing something.

Pause
He was deeply proud of his family, including the five siblings he grew up with and remained very close to throughout his life. He was a loving husband to my mother, Maggie, and a devoted grandfather. To Mae, Mollie, James, and Ali, he was simply “Dicky” — a constant presence, a source of constant kindness and fun and games.

Pause
We are grateful for the life he lived, for the laughter he brought, and for the example he set. We say goodbye today with love and thanks, and we wish him peace.
