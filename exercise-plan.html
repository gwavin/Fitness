8‑Week Workout Plan

Designed for: Gavin (46 y, 99 kg)
Duration: 8 weeks
Equipment: Squat rack with barbell (180 kg plates), bench, two sets of dumbbells, 20 kg kettlebell, resistance band, push‑up handles
Schedule: 45 minutes, 3 days a week

Key Principles

  • Shoulder Safety: Focus on shoulder-friendly exercises. Avoid overhead presses and any movements that cause pain.
  • Progressive Overload: Aim to increase reps or weight slightly each week to build strength.
  • Consistency: Stick to the schedule for the best results.

Weekly Structure

This program is designed to be completed in 45 minutes, 3 days a week, with a focus on full-body strength and conditioning. It avoids exercises that put direct strain on the shoulders, like overhead presses.

  • Weeks 1‑2: Technique & Mobility. Start with bodyweight and light kettlebell/dumbbell movements to groove the basics and work on range of motion.
  • Weeks 3‑4: Introduce Barbell Lifts. Begin light squats, bench press, and deadlifts in the rack. Focus on form—3 sets of 8 reps with moderate weight.
  • Weeks 5‑6: Add Intensity. Progress to 4‑5 sets of 5 reps on the barbell lifts, increasing the load gradually. Include accessory work like rows, lunges, and RDLs.
  • Weeks 7‑8: Strength Phase. Aim for heavier triples or sets of 5 while maintaining technique. Use the dumbbells and kettlebell for assistance exercises and conditioning finishers.

Evening Mobility Menu (pick 4‑5 nightly, rotate focus)

  • Scapular CARs – 5 each direction
  • Door‑frame pec stretch – 60 s/side
  • Thoracic cat‑camel – 1 min
  • Bretzel stretch – 1 min/side
  • Banded shoulder ER/IR – 2×20
  • Deep squat pry – 1 min total, move around
  • Ankle circles – 10 each direction per side
  • Wall calf stretch – 30 s/side

Progress & Safety Guidelines

Your health is the priority. Adhere to these rules strictly.
  • Pain scale rule: 0‑2/10 pain is acceptable during/after; >3 = modify the exercise or stop. Do not push through shoulder pain.
  • RPE diary: Jot down the RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion) after each main lift. If two sessions in a row feel >8, reduce the load or volume next time.
  • Reload week: If sleep, nutrition, or recovery dips, don't be afraid to repeat a previous, lighter week. Progress isn't always linear.
  • Protein target: Aim for 1.6‑1.8 g/kg bodyweight (~160‑180 g/day) to support muscle repair and growth.
  • Physio check‑in: Book a review with a professional if shoulder soreness lingers for more than 48 hours or if your overhead range of motion plateaus.

After Week 8

If your shoulder tolerates the new exercises well, you can consider gradually re-introducing more challenging movements. Always prioritize proper form and listen to your body.
