Week 8 – Final Leg Progression

Cap off the program with challenging squats, deadlifts and unilateral work.

Warm‑Up

Begin with 5–7 minutes of low‑impact activity like brisk walking or cycling followed by dynamic stretches for the shoulders and hips (arm circles, leg swings). Warming up increases circulation and prepares the muscles for exercise; research from the American Academy of Orthopaedic Surgeons suggests a 5–10‑minute low‑impact warm up before strengthening exercises and stretching afterwards【963587572219979†L210-L217】.

Workout (≈35 minutes)

  • Back Squat: 4 sets of 8 reps. Choose a sustainable weight and focus on depth and control.
  • Deadlift: 3 sets of 6 reps with moderate load. Keep the bar close and drive through heels.
  • Walking Lunge: 3 sets of 12 reps per leg. Hold dumbbells or kettlebells at your sides.
  • Single‑Leg Deadlift: 3 sets of 10 reps per leg using bodyweight or light weight. Balance and hinge at the hips.
  • Plank Variation: 3 sets of 60‑second holds if possible. Challenge yourself with plank variations (e.g., high plank, forearm plank).

Cooldown

Finish with about 5 minutes of gentle stretching (hamstrings, quadriceps, chest, and shoulders) to restore range of motion and reduce muscle soreness【963587572219979†L210-L217】.
